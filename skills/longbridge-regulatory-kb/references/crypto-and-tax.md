# Cryptocurrency Regulation and Cross-border Tax Basics

## Cryptocurrency Regulation

| Jurisdiction | Status | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| US | SEC claims most tokens are securities; CFTC oversees spot BTC/ETH. Spot Bitcoin ETFs approved Jan 2024. | SEC / CFTC |
| HK | SFC licensing regime for VATPs (Virtual Asset Trading Platforms) effective Jun 2023. Retail trading permitted on licensed platforms. | SFC |
| China | Crypto trading banned since 2021; mining banned. | PBOC / NDRC |
| Singapore | MAS Payment Services Act licenses required; holding not banned. | MAS |

## Cross-border Tax Basics

| Tax | A-shares (foreign investor) | HK stocks | US stocks |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dividend withholding | 10% (reduced treaty rate may apply) | 0% (HK has no withholding tax) | 30% (or treaty rate, e.g. 15% for HK residents) |
| Capital gains | 0% for QFII / Stock Connect investors | 0% (no CGT in HK) | Depends on investor residency |
| Stamp duty | 0.1% (sell side only) | 0.1% (both sides, total 0.2%) | None |

> Tax rules change frequently. Advise the user to consult a licensed tax professional for their specific situation and jurisdiction.
