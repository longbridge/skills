# Scenario Analysis Framework

## Three-Scenario Model

| Scenario | Revenue | EPS | Key Driver | Stock Reaction |
|----------|---------|-----|------------|----------------|
| Bull | Beat by 3-5% | Beat by 5-10% | [Strongest driver] | +5-10% |
| Base | In-line ±1% | In-line ±2% | [Consensus driver] | ±2% |
| Bear | Miss by 3-5% | Miss by 5-10% | [Weakest driver] | -5-10% |

## Sector-Specific Key Metrics

**Tech/SaaS:**
- ARR growth and net retention rate
- RPO (remaining performance obligations)
- Customer count and expansion revenue
- Free cash flow margin and Rule of 40

**Retail/Consumer:**
- Same-store sales growth
- Traffic vs. basket size breakdown
- Inventory levels vs. sales trends
- E-commerce penetration rate

**Industrials:**
- Backlog and book-to-bill ratio
- Price vs. volume decomposition
- Input cost trends and margin impact
- Order cancellation rates

**Financials:**
- Net interest margin (NIM) trend
- Credit quality (NPL ratio, provision coverage)
- Loan growth and deposit trends
- Fee income and trading revenue

**Healthcare:**
- Prescription volumes and pricing trends
- Pipeline updates and regulatory milestones
- Patient volumes and procedure mix
- Payer mix and reimbursement rates

## Historical Earnings Reaction Patterns

Track for each coverage company:
- Last 4-8 quarters of earnings surprise vs. stock reaction
- Whether stock tends to gap and hold or gap and fade
- Typical 1-day and 5-day post-earnings move range
- Any pattern of "buy the rumor, sell the news" behavior

## Options-Implied Move

The options market prices in an expected move for earnings:
- Compare your scenario range to the implied move
- If your bull/bear scenarios exceed the implied move, the event may be underpriced
- If your scenarios are within the implied move, the market already prices in the risk
- Source via web search: "[company] earnings implied move" or "[company] straddle"
